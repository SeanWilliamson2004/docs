1. In most cases, connect from Windows App to access your desktop.  See screenshot below.
2. You will each have your own desktop .   When you click connect for "UK Desktops" or the India equivalent, you will be connected to the desktop assigned to you.
3. The desktop should have the software you need: M365 Office, 1Password.
4. Support staff and consultants each also each have a Sage 200 server available that can be connected to.  When you click connect, you will be connected to the Sage Server assigned to you.
5. You can also connect to all Sage servers via rdp from your AVD Desktop.
6. You should NOT copy customers data onto your Sage servers.
7. Please shutdown your desktops and servers when you have finished working at the end of the day.
8. All desktops and Sage servers will shutdown in the evening, mostly at 18\.30\.  You should receive and email warning of this shutdown, and this email will have links that will allow you to delay the shutdown.
9. Clicking "Connect" should start the desktop or server.  This may take a little time at the start of the day, and it may be worth retrying if the connection times out.  We will also provide links to allow you to start them in Azure.
10. "Other Sage Servers" will hold other Sage servers.  These can be assigned to individuals (unfortunately, at the moment this requires IT).  You can rdp to any of them.
1. Other versions of Sage 200\.
2. A S1000 V4 2019 Hyper.  This can have customers' data on it.  All use of customers' data remains subject to the rules detailed in the Support handboolk
3. A "Customers Data" instance of Sage 200 2025R1\.  You can copy customers' data onto this machine.   All use of customers' data remains subject to the rules detailed in the Support handbook.

12. Like the Codis PCs, the AV PCs and servers are not backed up.  There are a number of cloud areas to use:
1. Work related data that isn't appropriate to be shared with your team \-  OneDrive
2. Work related data that isn't customer related such as team data \- Sharepoint
3. Customer related data \- CRM.  (For the most part.  There are other locations such as Repos).
4. Very large files such as VMs.  Azure Storage.

14. Desktops should have access to the same OneDrive folders that you could access from Codis based PCs.  They do not sync data from local folders as the Codis based ones generally did, but access the internet based data directly.
15. Note that access to all internet based resources and particularly Microsoft ones (OneDrive, DevOps, Azure Storage) is much faster than on the Codis PCs.
