Finance Sales Playbook

**Selling Codis Software Solutions to Finance Teams Using Sage 200**

## Overview

Codis provides software tools that enhance data entry and reporting for Sage 200 users, streamlining processes across the finance function. This playbook breaks down each finance team role, their core responsibilities and challenges, and how Codis products address their needs.

## Products at a Glance

- **Excelerator**: Excel\-to\-Sage 200 integration for fast, validated data entry.
- **Orchestrator**: Process automation and data flow control for Sage 200\.
- **Intellerator**: Pre\-built Power BI dashboards delivering reliable reporting from Sage 200\.

## Finance Team Roles \& Solutions

### 1\. Chief Financial Officer (CFO)

**Responsibilities:**

- Strategic financial leadership
- Reporting to stakeholders
- Budgeting, forecasting, risk management

**Challenges:**

- Delayed or unreliable data
- Poor forecasting accuracy
- Limited visibility across operations

**Codis Solutions:**

- **Intellerator**: Provides real\-time, trustworthy insights for strategic decisions.
- **Orchestrator**: Ensures clean, consistent data flows for reliable reporting.

### 

### 2\. Financial Controller / Head of Finance

**Responsibilities:**

- Financial control and compliance
- Monthly/annual reporting
- Audit preparation

**Challenges:**

- Manual processes leading to errors
- Bottlenecks during month\-end
- Disparate data across departments

**Codis Solutions:**

- **Excelerator**: Speeds up journal entries, invoices, and accruals into Sage 200\.
- **Intellerator**: Reduces time spent on reporting.
- **Orchestrator**: Streamlines recurring tasks and enforces control.

### 

### 3\. Finance Manager

**Responsibilities:**

- Managing finance operations
- Cash flow, payroll, AP/AR oversight
- Performance tracking

**Challenges:**

- Time\-consuming processes
- Manual data validation
- Team productivity monitoring

**Codis Solutions:**

- **Excelerator**: Reduces admin time via structured data import.
- **Intellerator**: Monitors team KPIs and financial trends.
- **Orchestrator**: Guides workflow and process consistency.

### 

### 4\. Management Accountant

**Responsibilities:**

- Management accounts and variance analysis
- Budgeting and internal reporting

**Challenges:**

- Data consolidation
- Risk of Excel\-based errors
- Repetitive reconciliation tasks

**Codis Solutions:**

- **Excelerator**: Clean, efficient data input into Sage.
- **Intellerator**: Reduces reporting build time with pre\-built dashboards.

### 

### 5\. Accounts Payable / Receivable Clerks

**Responsibilities:**

- Invoice processing and reconciliation
- Managing supplier and customer ledgers

**Challenges:**

- High\-volume data entry
- Manual invoice matching
- Risk of duplication or error

**Codis Solutions:**

- **Excelerator**: Import multiple transactions quickly and accurately.
- **Orchestrator**: Automates steps in invoice handling workflows.

### 

### 6\. Finance Systems Analyst / Sage Administrator

**Responsibilities:**

- Sage 200 maintenance and reporting
- Systems integration and optimisation

**Challenges:**

- Custom report development
- Manual data imports/exports

**Codis Solutions:**

- **Orchestrator**: Enables automated, controlled data flows.
- **Excelerator**: Validates and simplifies data imports.
- **Intellerator**: Removes the need for manual Power BI dashboard creation.

### 

### 7\. Finance Assistant / Junior Accountant

**Responsibilities:**

- Data entry, reconciliations, expense claims

**Challenges:**

- Inexperience with Sage 200
- High risk of manual errors

**Codis Solutions:**

- **Excelerator**: Easy\-to\-use Excel interface with validation reduces training time.
- Enables accurate processing of recurring tasks (journals, expenses).

## 

## Sales Tips

- Tailor the conversation to the **pain points** of each role.
- Lead with **Intellerator** for CFOs and Controllers; it's strategic.
- Promote **Excelerator** for operational roles; it's tactical and fast.
- Introduce **Orchestrator** where process control and automation are priorities.
