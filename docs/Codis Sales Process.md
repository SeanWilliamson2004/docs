This documents the Sales admin process from enquiry to sale. 

## Enquiry

An enquiry is when a customer asks us about a product they wish to buy. 

### Enquiry Details

All enquiries need to have the following information. 

1. Company Name
2. Company Website
3. Company Contact
4. Sage Accounting System
5. Telephone
6. Email
7. Source
8. Description of the enquiry.
9. Product Family
10. Sage Product Version Number
11. Sage Business Partner

### CRM

All enquiries need to be logged as opportunities in the CRM. Before we can log an opportunity we need to ensure that the company and the contact are on the CRM. 

Once we have been provided with more details regarding the End User's Information we need to open a direct Account for the End User. We will require the following information in order to open the account. 

1. Select the company
2. Select the person
3. Create the opportunity
4. Attach a Product Family
5. Opportunity Description a one line description
6. Opportunity Details store a much as detail as possible.
7. Opportunity source \- how the prospect heard of us.
8. Any communications likes email need to be filed against the opportunity

If the above information has not yet been obtained, the opportunity will need to be created under the Business Partner's Account. If however the end user is a direct customer we would need to open an account for them first hand. 

## Sales Qualification

Once an enquiry is logged on CRM it is passed to sales for qualification. 

## Presentation

If the prospect is deemed to be of interest to Codis \- the sales process commences. This usually involves a presentation of some kind. The presentation may require some discovery where the prospects needs are explored before the presentation. 

## Negotiation

Negotiation commences with a proposal (or quotation).   
Standard quotation documents are available on the wiki. 

Quotation terms to be decided before it is sent out. 

## Sale Closure

A sales is only recognised when the customer returns the signed Sage 50 Sales Order or the Customer's official Purchase Order. When the customer agrees to buy the Codis product we can close the opportunity. 

1. Close the opportunity in CRM
1. Update the opportunity Stage and Status
2. Update the date closed
3. Ensure that the number of licences and the number of consultancy days are filled in.
4. File the signed Sales order in the document section of the opportunity \- Document Type Signed\_Customer\_Order or Purchase\_Order.
5. Raise a task to contact the customer after the next month end for feedback or potential sales.

3. Where the order involves Codis product \- Licences
1. Create the Purchase Order in CRM \- Create it under the opportunity
2. Ensure that the Licence is activated made live
3. Double check the Expiry date for the licence

5. Where Codis are doing the implementation
1. Create the consultancy case on CRM. This link will explain [How to add consultancy case.](How To Add A Consultancy Case on Sage CRM.md)

7. Any 3rd party (or Sage) product need to be ordered.
1. Raise the Purchase Order in Sage 50 for the agreed amount supplied by the SalesRep.
2. Get the PO Authorised.
3. Send the PO to the supplier.

9. Documents to the end user/customer
1. Email a copy of the installation instructions along with the Licence IDs
2. Where it is a new customer send the customer a copy of the EULA for signing.

11. Producing a Sales invoice on Sage 50\.
1. If no order exists \- Create a Sage 50 Sales order.   
If an order exists \- remove any reference to quote and Expiry Date.
2. If the order does not include consultancy, produce the invoice and send to the customer.
3. However, if the invoice does involve consultancy and the payment terms are 'payment with order', raise the proforma for the deposit.
4. Once the work is done, convert the order into invoice.
5. Send the invoice to the customer.

13. Edit case on CRM.

                1\. Enter the Sales order number

                2\. Enter the Purchase order number

                3\. If we have raised the proform, set the status to 'Awaiting Payment' 

      8\. Update the whiteboard. 

## Historical

In Sage 50 Create the Sales Order /invoice. 

- When a new enquiry comes through this has to be logged into **CRM**. We do this by creating an account if they are a new customer/prospect.
- We need to obtain information such as their Full name, the name of their company, their contact number and their email address.
- Under the account we create an opportunity for the enquiry.

![](images/Sales_Sales_Wiki_Images_Sales_Process_opportunity_2.png) 

![](images/Sales_Sales_Wiki_Images_Sales_Process_Click_New_Opp.png) 

- We file relevant communication under the **'Communications'** tab under the opportunity, and the relevant documents such as Purchase Orders, Quotations, Signed Sales Orders, and EULAs under the **'Documents'** tab under the Opportunity using the 'Document Drop' function.

![](images/Sales_Sales_Wiki_Images_Sales_Process_Document_drop.png) 

![](images/Sales_Sales_Wiki_Images_Sales_Process_Communications_and_Documents.png) 

- You then need to alert the Head of Sales regarding this so they are aware of the Prospect/Customer.
- We need to make initial contact with the customer so we can speak to them about the Excelerator.
- We need to find out if they are a direct customer or if they have come via a Business Partner (Reseller)
- We can arrange a free **Webinar** via TeamViewer to give the customer an overview of the Excelerator and navigate them through the software's features and functionalities.
- We offer them a free 7\-14 days **Demo License** so they can familiarise themselves with the software and use it at their own leisure.
- We then discuss **pricing** (If they are a direct customer)and send over a quotation for their module(s) of choice. If they are via a reseller the quotation will need to be sent to the reseller.\*\*\*\*

### Conversion

- When we have received a new Purchase Order, we need to remember to close the opportunity by selecting **'Sales Closed'** and **'Won'**

![](images/Sales_Sales_Wiki_Images_Sales_Process_Sales_closed_and_Won.png) 

- The Purchase Order then needs to be processed on CRM, and then Sage 50\. Please see below for an Example of what details the Purchase Order holds.

![](images/Sales_Sales_Wiki_Images_Sales_Process_Annotated_Purchase_Order_as_Example_2.png) 

- Update the white board with the Excelerator Sales Figures
- The order needs to be processed under the End\-User's account. You need to make note of the **SO Reference** number and the **License ID(s)** on the hard copy of the Purchase Order.
- When the order has been processed on CRM,you need to make sure the Licenses have been configured to the correct **'Expiry date'**.

![](images/Sales_Sales_Wiki_Images_Sales_Process_Renewals.png) 

- The Licenses will need to be made **'Live'**

![](images/Sales_Sales_Wiki_Images_Sales_Process_Live_box.png) 

- The order then needs to be processed on Sage 50, so that we can generate a Sale Order and raise an invoice to the Customer/Reseller.
- If there is a **consultancy request**, we need to generate a Sales Order for this and send out an Invoice.

### Billing

- The Sales Order is then shown to Aman, Anna or BP for approval.
- When the Sales Order has been approved, the Sales Order is'Completed' and an Invoice is then generated and sent out to the Direct Customer, or Reseller.

![](images/Sales_Sales_Wiki_Images_Sales_Process_Sales_Order.png) 

![](images/Sales_Sales_Wiki_Images_Sales_Process_Invoice.png) 

### Delivery

- An email is then sent to the customer or reseller on how to install their Purchased Excelerator module(s)with installation links directing them to the Codis website.
- If they are a new customer we send an **End User License Agreement (EULA)** to them, requesting them to send a signed copy back to us. We explain to them that without this document we are in no position to issue out Licenses until we possess this document.
- Please see below an example of a completed EULA.

![](images/Sales_Sales_Wiki_Images_Sales_Process_Approved_EULA.png) 

### Follow up

- We contact the customer 2 weeks from the License issue date, and find out about their thoughts on the Excelerator.
- We can send them a module list of all the other available Excelerators compatible with their Sage version. Such as the **E\-Shot Sage 200 Exhibition Leaflet**, **Info sheets** and **Pricing.**
