As discussed together (Anna, Suraj \& Ricky), our approach for future requests from K3,
possibly for other business partners:

1. Business
 partner checking if the customer’s Excelerator licence has been extended
 and you don’t know whether they have checked internally with Finance
 first: "Have you had confirmation from your finance team when payment
 was made, the amount paid and whether it was a same day payment? Once you
 have checked internally, I can then look this up for you.”
2. Business
 partner requests a temporary extension if a customer has yet to pay them,
 or if the customer has paid in time but the partner hasn’t paid us by the
 renewal date: “I am no longer authorised to issue temporary extensions as
 standard in these type of situations. Once the Codis invoice relating to
 this customer has been paid, please allow 3\-5 working days between funds
 clearing on our side and the licence being activated.”
3. Customer
 requests a temporary extension if they have not paid in time, or if they
 did paid in time but the partner hasn’t paid us by the renewal date:
 “Please speak with your business partner in the first instance who can
 help you with this.”
4. If
 business partner says they did not receive the invoice: “Please find
 attached the email dated dd/mm/yyyy that shows the invoice sent to
 \[recipient]. Payment was due by dd/mm/yyyy.”
5. If
 Codis sends a late invoice or misses sending an invoice: “Sorry, we will
 send the invoice today and issue a temporary licence of x days while the
 payment clears.”
6. If
 the business partner places an urgent order, has provided proof of payment
 and requests an initial temp licence for the customer while funds clear:
 “While we are waiting for funds to clear and as this is an urgent order,
 we have issued a temporary licence to expire on dd/mm/yyyy. Once funds
 have cleared, we will issue the full year licence(s).”
7. Other rare situations where a payment hasn’t
 reached us in time and a temporary licence is requested: To be discussed
 on a case by case basis.



The above are drafts of what we can send for these type of scenarios. We
can continue to review and enhance these scenarios and responses over time.
